Elasticity of Supply

How responsive producers are to price changes

What is Elasticity of Supply?

Elasticity of supply measures the degree of responsiveness of quantity supplied to a change in price.

While demand elasticity focuses on consumer behaviour, supply elasticity reflects the flexibility of producers.

üìå Key question: How quickly and how much can producers increase output when price rises?

Price Elasticity of Supply (PES)

Price elasticity of supply is defined as:

üìê PES = (% Change in Quantity Supplied) √∑ (% Change in Price)

Supply is said to be elastic if producers can respond significantly to price changes, and inelastic if they cannot.

Arc Elasticity vs Point Elasticity (Supply)

Arc elasticity measures elasticity over a range of prices and quantities. It is used when price changes are large.

Point Elasticity of Supply

Point elasticity measures elasticity at a specific point on the supply curve. It is used when price changes are very small.

PES = (dQ / dP) √ó (P / Q)

üìå Unlike demand, supply elasticity is often constrained by production capacity.

Determinants of Elasticity of Supply

1. Time Period

Time is the most important determinant of supply elasticity.

  • Market Period: Perfectly inelastic supply
  • Short Run: Relatively inelastic supply
  • Long Run: More elastic supply

2. Nature of the Good

Perishable goods (e.g., milk, vegetables) tend to have inelastic supply, while manufactured goods tend to have more elastic supply.

3. Availability of Inputs

If inputs are easily available, supply is more elastic.

4. Flexibility of Production

Firms that can switch between products easily have more elastic supply.
