What is Market Welfare?

Market welfare refers to the overall well-being generated by a market. It depends on how efficiently goods are allocated between buyers and sellers.

Market Efficiency

A market outcome is efficient if it maximises total surplus.

  • Efficient markets allocate goods to buyers with highest WTP
  • Goods are produced by sellers with lowest cost

Deadweight Loss (DWL)

Deadweight loss is the reduction in total surplus caused by policies such as taxes, price ceilings, or price floors.

It occurs because some mutually beneficial trades do not take place.
