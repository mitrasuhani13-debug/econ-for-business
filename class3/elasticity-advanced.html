Point vs Arc Elasticity and Elasticity Along a Curve

Why Advanced Elasticity?

In earlier classes, elasticity was treated as a single number. In reality, elasticity varies depending on where you are on the demand curve.

üìå The same demand curve can be elastic at one point and inelastic at another.

Arc Elasticity vs Point Elasticity

Arc Elasticity

Arc elasticity measures elasticity over a **range** of prices and quantities. It is used when price changes are relatively large.

üìê Arc Elasticity Formula:
PED = (ŒîQ / Average Q) √∑ (ŒîP / Average P)

Point Elasticity

Point elasticity measures elasticity at a **specific point** on the demand curve. It is used when price changes are infinitesimally small.

üìê Point Elasticity Formula:
PED = (dQ / dP) √ó (P / Q)

Elasticity Along a Straight-Line Demand Curve

Consider a linear demand curve:

  • Upper portion ‚Üí Elastic demand

Elasticity and Total Revenue (Formal Logic)

Total Revenue (TR) is defined as:

TR = P √ó Q

  • If demand is elastic ‚Üí price and TR move in opposite directions
  • If demand is inelastic ‚Üí price and TR move in same direction
  • If demand is unitary elastic ‚Üí TR is maximum
