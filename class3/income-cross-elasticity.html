Income & Cross Elasticity of Demand

How demand responds to income and related goods

Why Study Other Elasticities?

Price is not the only factor affecting demand. Demand also responds to changes in consumer income and prices of related goods.

üìå Income and cross elasticities help firms predict market demand under changing economic conditions.

Income Elasticity of Demand (YED)

Income elasticity of demand measures the responsiveness of quantity demanded to a change in consumer income.

üìê Formula:
YED = (% Change in Quantity Demanded) √∑ (% Change in Income)

Types of Goods Based on Income Elasticity

Normal Goods (YED > 0)

Demand increases as income increases.

  • Food
  • Clothing
  • Electronics

Inferior Goods (YED < 0)

Demand decreases as income increases.

  • Low-quality cereals
  • Second-hand goods

Necessities vs Luxuries

  • Necessities: YED between 0 and 1 (e.g., basic food, electricity)
  • Luxuries: YED > 1 (e.g., cars, vacations, premium phones)

üìå As income grows, spending on luxuries rises faster than income.

Numerical Example (Income Elasticity)

Income increases by 20% and quantity demanded increases by 10%.

YED = 10% √∑ 20% = 0.5

Since YED < 1, the good is a necessity.

Cross Elasticity of Demand (XED)

Cross elasticity of demand measures the responsiveness of quantity demanded of one good to a change in the price of another good.

üìê Formula:
XED = (% Change in Quantity of Good A) √∑ (% Change in Price of Good B)

Types of Goods Based on Cross Elasticity

Substitutes (XED > 0)

An increase in the price of one good increases demand for the other.

  • Tea and coffee
  • Butter and margarine

Complements (XED < 0)

An increase in the price of one good decreases demand for the other.

  • Cars and petrol
  • Printers and ink cartridges

Unrelated Goods (XED = 0)

Change in price of one good does not affect demand for the other.

  • Books and bread

Numerical Example (Cross Elasticity)

Price of coffee rises by 10%, and demand for tea increases by 15%.

XED = 15% √∑ 10% = +1.5

Tea and coffee are substitutes.

Why Income & Cross Elasticity Matter for Business

  • Demand forecasting
  • Product positioning
  • Competitive strategy
  • Targeting luxury vs mass markets
