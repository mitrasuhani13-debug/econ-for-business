What is Economics?

Economics is the study of how individuals, businesses, and societies allocate scarce resources to satisfy unlimited wants.

It examines decision-making under constraints and explains how choices are made in the presence of scarcity.

Scarcity

Scarcity is the fundamental economic problem. It arises because human wants are unlimited, while resources such as time, money, labour, and natural resources are limited.

Because of scarcity, not all wants can be satisfied simultaneously. This forces individuals and firms to make decisions.

Choice

Due to scarcity, every economic agent must make choices. Choosing one option automatically means giving up another.

Choices are influenced by preferences, income, prices, and expectations. In business, choices are guided by cost-benefit analysis.

ðŸ“Œ Example: A firm choosing to invest in marketing may postpone investment in new machinery.

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative that is forgone when a choice is made.

It represents the true cost of any decision and plays a crucial role in both personal and business decision-making.

ðŸ“Œ Example: If a student spends an evening watching a movie instead of studying, the opportunity cost is the learning or grades forgone.

Why Study Economics for Business?

Economics provides a framework for understanding markets, consumer behaviour, production decisions, and pricing strategies.

  • Helps firms decide optimal prices
  • Aids in demand and cost forecasting
  • Explains market competition
  • Guides policy and strategic decisions

ðŸ“Œ Example: Understanding demand elasticity helps firms decide whether raising prices will increase or decrease revenue.

Microeconomics and Macroeconomics

Economics is broadly divided into two branches: Microeconomics and Macroeconomics.

Microeconomics

Microeconomics studies individual economic units such as consumers, firms, and specific markets.

Macroeconomics

Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole, including inflation, unemployment, economic growth, and national income.
