Elasticity

Measuring responsiveness in economics

What is Elasticity?

Elasticity measures the degree of responsiveness of one economic variable to a change in another variable.

In microeconomics, elasticity is mainly used to measure how quantity demanded or supplied responds to changes in price.

üìå Elasticity answers: How sensitive is quantity to price?

Price Elasticity of Demand (PED)

Price elasticity of demand measures the responsiveness of quantity demanded to changes in price.

üìê PED = (% Change in Quantity Demanded) √∑ (% Change in Price)

The value of PED helps firms and governments understand consumer behaviour.
