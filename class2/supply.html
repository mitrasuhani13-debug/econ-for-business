Supply

Understanding producer behaviour

Meaning of Supply

Supply refers to the quantity of a good or service that producers are willing and able to offer for sale at various prices during a given period of time.

Unlike demand, supply reflects the behaviour of firms and producers.

üìå Key idea: Willingness + ability to sell = supply

Law of Supply

The law of supply states that, ceteris paribus, the quantity supplied of a good increases when its price increases and decreases when its price decreases.

üìå Reason: Higher prices make production more profitable.

Supply Schedule and Supply Curve

A supply schedule shows quantities supplied at different prices. Plotting these combinations gives the supply curve.

The supply curve slopes upward from left to right, showing a direct relationship between price and quantity supplied.

Supply Curve: Movement vs Shift (Interactive)

Changing price causes a movement along the supply curve. Changing costs or technology causes a shift of the supply curve.

Determinants of Supply

  • Cost of production
  • Technology
  • Prices of inputs
  • Number of sellers
  • Government policy (taxes, subsidies)
  • Expectations of future prices

üìå Example: Technological improvement shifts supply to the right.

  • Guides cost management
  • Explains producer response to price changes
